History & Philosophy

The Zen Bei Butoku Kai (All American Martial Virtues Association) was founded by O'Sensei Richard Kim in 1959. It is the North American branch of the Dai Nippon Butoku Kai, originally established in Kyoto, Japan, in 1895.

Founding & Lineage

Established in San Francisco, the Zen Bei Butoku Kai was created to preserve the comprehensive martial traditions of Japan and Okinawa. O'Sensei Kim served as the international representative of the original Dai Nippon Butoku Kai, ensuring that the virtues and techniques were transmitted authentically to the West.

Our Mission

The mission of the Zen Bei Butoku Kai is to preserve the traditional martial arts and to foster international goodwill and understanding through the exchange of martial culture.

Disciplines Preserved

O'Sensei Kim's teachings were comprehensive, encompassing a wide range of traditional fighting arts:

Shorinji Ryu Karate

A dynamic striking art rooted in Okinawan and Japanese traditions, emphasizing speed, power, and practical self-defense.

Daito Ryu Aiki-jujutsu

The grandfather of Aikido. An internal grappling art focusing on joint locks, throws, and neutralizing an opponent's energy.

Kobudo

The ancient art of Okinawan weaponry, including the Bo (staff), Sai, Tonfa, Kama, and Nunchaku.

Tai Chi Chuan

Internal training for health, balance, and developing internal power (Ki).

The Butoku (Martial Virtues)

  • Rei - Respect
  • Gi - Justice
  • Yu - Courage
  • Jin - Benevolence
  • Makoto - Sincerity
  • Meiyo - Honor
  • Chugi - Loyalty
